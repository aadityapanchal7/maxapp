# Posture & Visual Fitmaxxing

Posture changes how your body is perceived instantly. A trained posture makes the same physique look taller, broader, leaner, and more athletic. Bad posture makes a great body look worse than it is.

## Why Posture Matters

Good posture makes you look:
- Taller
- More confident
- Broader (shoulders square)
- Leaner (long torso line)
- More athletic
- More capable

Common postural problems most lifters fight:
- Forward head
- Rounded shoulders
- Slouched upper back ("kyphotic")
- Anterior pelvic tilt
- Weak upper back relative to chest
- Weak glutes / core

## Muscles That Drive Posture

Train these to fix the look automatically:
- Rear delts
- Rhomboids
- Mid traps
- Lower traps
- Glutes
- Core (lightly, for stability)
- Neck extensors

## Posture-Support Exercises

- Face pulls — 3×15 at the end of every push session
- Reverse pec deck — 3×12–15
- Chest-supported rows — 3×8–12
- Cable rows — 3×10–12
- Back extensions — 2×12–15
- Dead hangs — 2 × 30 seconds
- Neck extension work (light, controlled)
- Glute bridges — 2×12
- Planks — 2 × 30–60 sec

## Daily Postural Cues

Practice these all day, not just in the gym:
- Shoulders relaxed and slightly back (not "military" stiff)
- Chin neutral, slightly tucked
- Ribcage stacked over pelvis (no rib flare)
- Feet planted evenly when standing
- No exaggerated arching of the lower back

Good posture should look natural, not forced.

## Clothing — Visual Fitmaxxing

Clothes either show what you've built or hide it.

### Shirt Fit Rules
- Sleeve hugs the arm slightly (not loose, not painted on)
- Shoulder seam sits on your actual shoulder (not below)
- Chest has some structure
- Waist not baggy
- Fabric not so thin it shows everything

### Best Shirt Styles
- Fitted black tee
- Ribbed tank
- Henley
- Athletic-fit tee
- Cropped boxy tee (if you have a small waist)

### Avoid
- Sleeves that flare out or end below the elbow
- Shirts too tight around the stomach
- Baggy torso fits that hide your width
- Thin clingy fabric if you're not lean

### Sleeves
- Should end around mid-bicep
- A slight roll can make arms look bigger
- Don't wear sleeves that are too long if your goal is arm emphasis

### Jackets
Best options: bomber, denim, leather, overshirt, structured cropped jacket.

Why they work: add shoulder structure, create upper-body width, hide waist softness, improve silhouette.

Layering trick: fitted shirt under structured jacket = maximum perceived width.

### Pants
Best: athletic taper, straight fit, relaxed taper, room in thighs with clean ankle taper.
Avoid: super skinny jeans (look weak on developed legs), extremely baggy (shapeless), pants that swallow your legs.

### Shoes
Slight platform sneakers, chunkier sneakers, clean white sneakers, structured boots.

Tiny shoes unbalance a developed upper body.

### Color Tricks
- Dark colors slim the waist, hide softness, create cleaner lines
- Light upper body makes chest/shoulders pop
- Monochrome outfits read taller and more put-together
