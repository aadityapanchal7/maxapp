# Body Fat & Leanness

Body fat is the master variable. A 6 with structure shows up. A 9 hidden under fat doesn't. But going too lean has its own costs — the goal is leaner, full, strong, and sustainable, not "shredded at all costs."

## Why Body Fat Matters

Higher body fat:
- Blurs muscle definition
- Hides the jawline
- Softens facial features
- Widens the visual waist
- Wrecks how clothes hang

Very low body fat:
- Looks depleted, flat, tired
- Tanks energy, sleep, training performance
- Disrupts hormones and mood
- Hard to maintain socially

## Ideal Aesthetic Range

For most men: ~12–15% body fat.

At this range:
- Abs lightly visible without flexing
- Face sharper, jawline visible
- Muscles read through normal clothing
- Waist stays tight
- Energy stays high
- Sustainable year-round

Going below 10% is optional. Looks great in photos, hard to maintain, not necessary for "looking jacked in clothes."

## Cutting — The Math

1. Estimate maintenance calories (bodyweight in lbs × 14–16, less if sedentary)
2. Eat 300–500 below maintenance
3. Track 7-day average bodyweight (weigh daily, look at the trend, ignore the day-to-day noise)
4. Adjust calories down by 100–150 if no movement after 2 weeks
5. Keep protein at 0.8–1 g/lb
6. Keep lifting heavy
7. Walk 8–10k steps/day

Expect 0.5–1% bodyweight fat loss per week. Faster than that and you're losing muscle.

## How To Cut Without Looking Worse

- Keep lifting heavy — don't switch to high-rep "toning"
- Keep carbs in the diet
- Don't crash diet
- Sleep enough
- Keep sodium and water consistent (don't yo-yo)
- Maintain protein

If your face looks tired, your pumps disappear, and your workouts collapse, you cut too aggressively. Add 100–200 calories back.

## Maintenance Strategy

A maintenance phase between cuts isn't optional — it's how you protect muscle and metabolism. Spend 2–4 weeks at maintenance after every 6–8 weeks of cutting. Mood, sleep, performance all rebound; the next cut works better.

## Key Takeaway

The best-looking body is rarely the leanest body. It's the one that's lean, full, strong, and sustainable.
