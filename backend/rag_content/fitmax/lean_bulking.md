# Lean Bulking & Gaining Weight

A lean bulk is a small calorie surplus aimed at gaining muscle with minimal fat. Done right, you fill out clothes and your face stays sharp. Done wrong, your waist and face get worse and the next cut is brutal.

## When To Bulk

Consider gaining weight if:
- You're underweight for your height
- You lack visible muscle even when lean
- Strength has been stalled for 6+ weeks
- You look skinny even in fitted clothes
- Your frame needs filling out (especially shoulders, lats, upper chest)

## Lean Bulk Math

- Surplus: maintenance + 200–300 calories
- Expected gain: ~0.25–0.5 lb per week
- If you gain >1 lb/week consistently, most of it is fat — drop the surplus by 100 cal
- Track 7-day average bodyweight; ignore daily fluctuations

A small surplus is enough. Aggressive surpluses don't grow muscle faster — they grow fat faster.

## Good Bulking Foods

Calorie-dense and digestible:

- Rice with sauce (calorie-dense, easy to eat)
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel — protein + healthy fats)
- Whole eggs
- Less-lean meats (80/20 ground beef)
- Pasta with olive oil and protein
- Smoothies (oats + banana + peanut butter + whey + milk)
- Olive oil drizzled on meals
- Greek yogurt
- Cream of rice
- Nut butters (in moderation — calorie bomb)

Add a side of greens for digestion.

## Appetite Hacks (when you struggle to eat enough)

- Use liquid calories (smoothies, milk)
- Lower-volume foods (pasta, rice, oils)
- Add sauces — calories without the volume
- Walk after meals to speed digestion
- Don't overload fiber — fills you up before calories hit
- Spread meals into 5–6 smaller ones

## The Dirty Bulk Trap

Dirty bulking (eating "anything that fits") usually leads to:
- Face fat (the part nobody warns you about)
- Worse skin (insulin spikes, processed food)
- Bigger waist (V-taper destroyed)
- Confidence crash mid-bulk
- Brutal multi-month cut afterward

The lean bulk takes longer per pound of muscle but leaves you looking better the entire way.

## Sample Lean Bulk Day (~180 lb male, ~2750 cal)

**Breakfast**: 4 whole eggs + 80g oats + banana + 30g whey
**Lunch**: 6oz chicken thigh + 1.5 cup rice + olive oil + greens
**Pre-workout**: 30g whey + 50g cream of rice + honey
**Post-workout**: 6oz lean ground beef + 1.5 cup rice + greens
**Dinner**: 6oz salmon + sweet potato + greens + olive oil
**Before bed**: 1 cup Greek yogurt + 1 tbsp peanut butter

## When To Stop Bulking

- Body fat creeping above 17–18%
- Face visibly softer in photos
- Waist measurement up >1 inch
- Strength stops responding to the surplus

Switch to maintenance for 2–3 weeks, then start a short cut. Bulk-cut cycles are normal; chronic bulking past 18% is usually a mistake.
