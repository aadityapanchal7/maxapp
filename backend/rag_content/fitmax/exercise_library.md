# Exercise Library

A short list of high-ROI exercises per body part. Pick 1–2 per area, run them for at least 6–8 weeks, and progress them. Variety is overrated; progression is everything.

## Chest

### Upper Chest (priority for aesthetics)
- Low-incline dumbbell press
- Incline Smith machine press
- Incline machine press

Cues: low incline (15–30°), shoulder blades stable, control the stretch, don't turn it into a front delt move.

### Mid Chest
- Pec deck
- Machine press
- Cable fly

Cues: tension on the chest the whole time, no bouncing, pause in the stretch, squeeze without losing shoulder position.

### Lower Chest
- Dips
- Cable cross-under fly

Cues: lean slightly forward on dips; avoid shoulder pain; control the bottom.

## Back

### Lat Width
- Neutral-grip pulldown
- Pull-ups
- Cable lat prayer pulldown

Cues: depress the scapula, drive elbows down, don't pull with your hands, no swinging.

### Mid-Back Thickness
- Chest-supported row
- Machine row
- Cable row

Cues: chest supported when possible, pull elbows back, pause at contraction, no momentum.

### Rear Delts
- Reverse pec deck
- Cable rear delt fly
- Single-arm reverse pec deck

Cues: light weight, lead with elbows, don't turn it into a trap movement.

### Lower Back / Posterior Chain
- Back extensions
- RDLs
- Controlled hinges

Cues: hinge at the hips, neutral spine, control the stretch in the hamstrings.

## Shoulders

### Side Delts (highest ROI)
- Cable lateral raise
- Behind-the-back cable lateral raise
- Lean-away lateral raise
- Machine lateral raise

Cues: lead with elbows, traps relaxed, no momentum, control the negative.

### Front Delts
- Shoulder press
- Machine shoulder press

Don't over-prioritize — they get plenty of work from any pressing.

## Arms

### Biceps
- Preacher curl
- Bayesian curl
- Incline dumbbell curl
- Cable curl

### Triceps
- Cable pushdown
- Overhead cable extension (long head — important for arm size)
- Skullcrushers
- Dips

The long head of the triceps is the biggest visual win. Train it directly with overhead extensions.

## Legs

### Quads
- Hack squat
- Leg press
- Pendulum squat
- Leg extension

### Hamstrings
- Lying leg curl
- Seated leg curl
- RDL

### Calves
- Standing calf raise
- Seated calf raise
- Single-leg calf raise

Train calves heavy, slow tempo, full stretch. They're stubborn and need real intent.

### Glutes (proportion balance)
- Hip thrust
- Bulgarian split squat
- Cable kickback

## Neck (carefully)

- Neck curls (lying on back, head off bench)
- Neck extensions (face down, head off bench)
- Side isometrics

Light weight, slow tempo, 2–3×/week max. Stop immediately if dizzy or numb.

## Core (lightly, for support)

- Cable crunch
- Hanging leg raise
- Planks

Don't train obliques heavy — boxy waist kills the V-taper.
