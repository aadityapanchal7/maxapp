# Workout Splits & Programming

Your split matters less than people think. What matters more: weekly volume, recovery, exercise quality, progressive overload, consistency, and training close to failure.

## The Core Principle

Most muscles grow well with ~10–12 hard sets per muscle per week. These can be spread across days or stacked into one — frequency matters less than total quality volume.

Example: 6 chest sets Monday + 6 chest sets Thursday usually beats 12 sets in one Monday session because the quality of each set is higher when you're fresh.

## Recovery Window

Most muscles recover in 48–72 hours, depending on:
- Training intensity (closer to failure = longer recovery)
- Sleep
- Nutrition
- Stress
- Exercise selection (compound vs isolation)
- Individual recovery ability

Hit a muscle again before it recovers → performance drops. Wait too long between sessions → frequency benefit is lost.

## The Best Splits

### 3-Day Full Body
Best for: beginners, busy people, learning exercises.

```
Mon: Full Body
Wed: Full Body
Fri: Full Body
```

Benefits: high frequency per muscle, easy recovery, simple to follow.

### 4-Day Upper / Lower
Best for: beginners to intermediates who want structure.

```
Mon: Upper
Tue: Lower
Wed: Rest
Thu: Upper
Fri: Lower
Sat–Sun: Rest
```

Benefits: great recovery, easy progression, balanced development.

### Push / Pull / Legs (PPL)
Best for: people who like training often, intermediate lifters, higher volume.

- Push: chest, shoulders, triceps
- Pull: back, rear delts, biceps
- Legs: quads, hamstrings, calves, glutes

Run as PPL/PPL/Rest (6 days) or PPL/Rest/PPL/Rest (5 days).

### Arnold Split (Chest+Back / Shoulders+Arms / Legs)
Best for: high-frequency training, shorter focused sessions.

### Full Body Every Other Day
Best for: people who can manage recovery. Each muscle trained often, great skill practice, less time between stimulus.

## Splits To Be Cautious With

### Bro Split (one muscle per week)
```
Mon: chest, Tue: back, Wed: shoulders, Thu: arms, Fri: legs
```

Can work, but typically less efficient for natural lifters because each muscle waits 7 days between stimuli — frequency benefit lost.

## Volume Guidelines

| Goal | Sets/muscle/week |
|------|------------------|
| Maintenance | 6–10 |
| Growth | 10–16 |
| Specialization | 16–22 (short blocks only) |

More than 22 hard sets per muscle per week usually backfires unless you're specializing for a short block and dropping volume elsewhere.

## Progression Rules

- Pick a small number of main lifts and progress them slowly
- Add weight when you can hit the top of your rep range with good form, two sessions in a row
- Track in a notes app, spreadsheet, or notebook — if you don't log, you don't progress
- Don't rotate exercises constantly; you need 4–8 weeks on the same lifts to see progression curves

## Key Takeaway

The best split is the one you can run consistently. Pick the structure that fits your week and grind. Switching every 4 weeks is the #1 killer of natural progress.
