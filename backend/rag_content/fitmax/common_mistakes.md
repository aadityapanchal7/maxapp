# Common Mistakes

A list of patterns that hold most lifters back. The fixes are usually simple; the discipline to apply them is the harder part.

## Training Mistakes

### Changing Exercises Too Often
If you change exercises every 2–3 weeks, you can't track progression. Stick with the same lifts for 6–8 weeks minimum. The boredom is the price of admission.

### Too Much Junk Volume
More sets are not always better. Sets only count if they're hard, controlled, and recoverable. 8 hard sets beats 16 mediocre ones.

### Ego Lifting
Heavy weight with bad form reduces tension on the target muscle and increases injury risk. The mirror sees what the muscle did, not what the weight said.

### Ignoring Side Delts and Lats
These two muscles drive perceived width more than any others. If your goal is aesthetics and you're not training them directly with high frequency, you're capping your physique.

### Ignoring Legs
A big upper body on twigs reads incomplete in shorts. Legs don't have to be massive — they have to be balanced.

### Skipping Long-Head Triceps
The long head is what makes arms look big from the side. If you only do pushdowns, you're missing the biggest visual win. Add overhead extensions.

### Cardio That Crushes Lifting
Long aggressive cardio sessions trash recovery. Walking 8–10k steps per day beats 45 min on the stairmaster for most lifters.

## Diet Mistakes

### Dirty Bulking
Fast weight gain = mostly fat. Your face gets soft, waist creeps up, and the next cut is brutal.

### Cutting Too Fast
Lose more than 1% bodyweight per week and you lose muscle, fullness, and strength. The sustainable cut is the better-looking cut.

### Avoiding Carbs
Carbs fuel performance and keep muscles full. Low-carb diets work for some people for some goals — usually not for hypertrophy.

### Not Eating Enough Protein
0.7–1 g/lb is the floor. Skip this and you waste training stimulus.

### Snacking Without Tracking
"Tastes" and "bites" add up. The fastest way to stall a cut is honest tracking that ignores the bites.

### Liquid Calories You Forget
Coffee with cream and sugar, sports drinks, beer, smoothies — easy to slot in 500+ uncounted calories per day.

## Lifestyle Mistakes

- Bad sleep (the master variable)
- Too much caffeine late
- Poor hydration
- No rest days
- Bad hygiene
- Not walking enough
- Stress that nobody is addressing

## Aesthetic Mistakes

- Only training chest and arms (top-heavy, weak frame)
- Ignoring posture
- Wearing bad-fitting clothes
- Letting waist get too big during a bulk
- Never training neck, calves, or rear delts
- Excessive direct ab/oblique work (boxy waist)
- Skipping the scale check for weeks ("I'll just feel it out")

## The Meta-Mistake

Inconsistency. The lifter who runs a mediocre program perfectly for 2 years beats the lifter who runs perfect programs for 6 weeks at a time.

Pick a plan. Run it 12 weeks minimum. Track. Adjust. Repeat.
