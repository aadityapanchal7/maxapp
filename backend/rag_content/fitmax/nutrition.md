# Nutrition

Your diet should make you look better and train better. If your diet leaves you weak, flat, bloated, or inconsistent, it's the wrong diet — regardless of what the macros say.

## Protein

Drives muscle growth, recovery, and muscle retention while dieting.

- General target: ~0.7 g per lb bodyweight daily
- Aggressive cut: ~1 g per lb
- Distribute across 3–5 meals, each with at least 30g (or 0.3 g/kg)

Good sources: chicken, lean ground beef, Greek yogurt, egg whites, whey, turkey, fish, cottage cheese.

Protein also kills hunger — high-protein meals are easier to diet on.

## Carbs

Carbs are not the enemy. They're one of the most important tools for performance and muscle fullness.

What carbs do:
- Refill glycogen
- Drive pumps
- Maintain strength
- Fuel training intensity
- Speed recovery
- Keep muscles full and round

Best timing: pre-workout, post-workout, around hard training days.

Good sources: rice, potatoes, oats, cream of rice, fruit, low-fat cereal, tortillas.

Low-carb diets make you flatter, weaker, and worse-looking if you're hypertrophy-focused. Don't cut carbs unless there's a specific reason.

## Fats

Support hormones, satiety, and general health. But calorie-dense — easy to overeat.

Good sources: eggs, olive oil, avocado, fatty fish, nuts (controlled portions).

Don't eliminate fats. Just measure them — a "spoonful" of nut butter is usually 200+ calories.

## Ideal Macro Setup

For most aesthetic muscle-building phases:
- High carb
- Medium protein
- Medium-to-low fat

This fuels training while keeping you lean.

## Sample Days

### Maintenance day for ~180 lb male
- Protein: 150g
- Carbs: 280g
- Fat: 70g
- Total: ~2350 cal

### Cut day, same person
- Protein: 180g
- Carbs: 200g
- Fat: 55g
- Total: ~1900 cal

### Lean bulk day
- Protein: 165g
- Carbs: 350g
- Fat: 80g
- Total: ~2780 cal

Adjust based on activity, age, and how the scale moves over 7-day averages.

## Meal Timing

- Train fed (carbs 60–120 min before)
- Post-workout: protein + carbs within an hour or two — useful but not magic
- Last meal 2–3 h before bed (better sleep, less reflux)
- Spread protein across the day rather than dumping it in one meal

## Hydration

2–3 L water/day baseline. Add 500 ml per hour of intense training, more in heat. Add electrolytes (sodium + potassium) if you sweat heavily.

## Key Takeaway

Your diet should fuel your physique, not fight it. If your training output is dropping, your face looks tired, or you're constantly hungry — something's off. Audit.
