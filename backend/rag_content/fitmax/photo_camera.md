# Photo & Camera Fitmaxxing

Most "transformation" content online is 70% better photography. Same body, better angle and lighting, +30% perceived size. Learn the variables you control.

## Pre-Photo Routine ("Pumpmaxxing")

Right before photos or content:
- Push-ups (2–3 sets of 10–15) — chest, front delts pop
- Lateral raises (banded or light DBs, 2 sets of 12–15) — shoulders fill out
- Curls (2 sets) — arms vascular and full
- 200–400ml water 30–60 min before — fuller muscles, not bloated
- Light carbs ~90 min before (banana, rice cakes, oats)
- Good lighting source identified

Don't overdo it — you want to look enhanced, not sweaty and desperate.

## Looking Bigger On Camera

Camera angle changes perceived physique dramatically.

To look bigger:
- Slightly lower angle (camera at chest height or just below)
- Shoulders pulled back, lats flared
- Slight torso twist (3/4 rotation)
- Arms slightly away from the body — never pinned to the side
- Limb closer to camera looks bigger (bicep flex toward lens)
- Even lighting from the side or front-side

## Best Angles

- Mirror angle around chest height or slightly lower
- Side chest angle (3/4 rotation)
- Back/lat width angle (lats flared, slight forward lean)
- Walking shot in fitted clothes (motion = athletic)
- Slight torso rotation (never directly square)

## Bad Angles

- Camera too high (childlike, loses chest)
- Straight-down selfie (tiny shoulders, big head)
- Flat front-facing flash (no shadow = no shape)
- Slouched relaxed posture
- Wide-angle lens up close (distorts proportions)

## Lighting

Lighting matters as much as the pump.

Good lighting:
- Slight overhead lighting (gym lighting works perfectly for this)
- Side lighting at 45° (sculpts shoulders, arms, chest)
- Natural window light from an angle
- Golden-hour outdoor light

Bad lighting:
- Flat front light (washes everything out)
- Harsh bathroom lighting from the wrong direction
- Dark room with no contrast
- Direct flash at the body

## Posing Basics

Simple rules that translate to "natural and athletic" rather than "weird gym selfie":
- Shoulders back, lats slightly flared
- Chest up naturally — don't force it like a bodybuilding pose in a Target parking lot
- Chin neutral
- Core lightly braced (don't hold breath, just engage)
- Arms slightly out from the body
- Don't press arms into your sides — destroys lat width

## Phone Camera Limitations

Phone front cameras are wide-angle and distort. For serious physique photos:
- Use the back camera
- Have someone else take it
- Stand 6–10 feet from the camera, not arm's length
- 50mm-equivalent lens or longer = most natural proportions

## When To Take Photos

- 3–4 hours after waking, after light eating
- Pre-workout (pumped from warmups, not flat from cardio)
- Morning of a low-sodium day
- Skip post-cheat-meal photos — you'll look puffy and demoralized
