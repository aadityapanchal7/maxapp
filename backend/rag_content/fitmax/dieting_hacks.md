# Dieting Hacks

The goal of dieting is to lose fat while keeping muscle, strength, and fullness. A good cut leaves you sharper. A bad cut leaves you smaller and dead-looking.

## High-Volume / Low-Calorie Foods

These work because they let you eat real volumes of food without spiking calories:

- Rice cakes
- Diet soda
- Greek yogurt (Oikos Triple Zero, etc.)
- Egg whites
- Low-carb tortillas
- Lean ground beef (90/10 or leaner)
- Protein ice cream (Halo Top, Nick's, etc.)
- Chicken breast
- Vegetables (huge volume, ~30 cal/cup)
- Fruit (filling, fiber, micronutrients)

## Hunger Control Tools

- **Morning caffeine** suppresses appetite for 2–3 hours
- **Chewing gum** between meals (sugar-free, watch for sorbitol stomach upset)
- **High-fiber foods** at every meal — psyllium, vegetables, oats
- **Carbonated drinks** — fills the stomach with no calories
- **Last meal close to bedtime** — prevents waking hungry
- **Volumize meals** — same calories spread across more food = more satiety

A lot of dieting "discipline" is just not letting yourself get to extreme hunger.

## Weekly Calorie Budgeting

You don't need every day to be identical. Banking calories for social meals beats white-knuckling perfect adherence.

Example for a 1900 cal/day target:

| Day | Calories |
|-----|----------|
| Mon–Fri | 1750 |
| Saturday | 2650 (social meal) |
| Sunday | 1900 |
| **Weekly avg** | **~1900** |

The weekly average is what drives fat loss. Daily is noise.

## Cutting Without Looking Worse

A bad cut wrecks your appearance:
- Face tired and sunken
- Muscles flat, no fullness
- Workouts collapse, strength drops
- Mood and sleep tank

To prevent it:
- Keep carbs in (~40–50% of total calories minimum)
- Keep sodium consistent — don't yo-yo
- Drink enough water (2.5–3 L)
- Don't lose more than 1% bodyweight per week
- Keep lifting heavy at the same rep ranges
- Sleep 7–9 hours

If your workouts are collapsing, you cut too aggressively. Add 100–200 cal back, rebuild performance for a week, then continue.

## Diet Breaks

Every 6–8 weeks of cutting, take 1–2 weeks at maintenance.

Why:
- Hormones rebound (leptin, T3, testosterone)
- Mood and sleep improve
- Strength returns
- Next cut works better
- Sustainability

Maintenance week math: bodyweight × 14–16. Eat to maintain.

## Pre-Refeed Trick (1-day version)

If the diet feels especially flat:
- 1-day +30% calories from carbs (not fat)
- Keep protein the same
- Don't add liquid calories
- You'll wake up fuller, with better pumps

Use sparingly — once every 2–3 weeks max during a long cut.
