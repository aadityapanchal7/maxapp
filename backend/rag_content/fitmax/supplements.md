# Supplements

Supplements are support tools, not magic. They help around the edges with energy, recovery, hydration, and protein intake. They cannot replace training hard, eating enough protein, sleeping, progressive overload, and consistency.

## Tier 1 — Real Evidence

### Creatine Monohydrate
The most-studied supplement in sports nutrition.

- 3–5g daily, every day
- Loading not necessary (5g/day for a few weeks reaches saturation)
- Pulls water into muscle (good — fuller, stronger), not into the face
- Take with any meal; timing doesn't matter
- Doesn't cause hair loss in normal doses (the meta-analyses don't support it)

### Whey / Protein Powder
Just convenient food. Useful when:
- You struggle to hit your protein target from food
- You need a fast meal
- Post-workout when whole food isn't available
- Mixed with oats or cream of rice for an easy meal

Not mandatory. If you can hit 0.7–1 g/lb from food, you don't need it.

### Caffeine
Improves training output, focus, perceived effort.

- 100–300mg ~30–60 min before training
- Last cup before noon if you train in the morning, before 4pm latest if afternoon
- Sleep matters more than one hyped workout — protect bedtime

### Electrolytes
Useful when sweating heavily, training in heat, or eating low sodium.

- Sodium + potassium are the main two
- LMNT, Liquid IV (sugar-aware), or DIY (1/4 tsp salt + lemon)

## Tier 2 — Situationally Useful

### Vitamin D3 (+ K2)
- 1000–2000 IU/day if sun exposure is low
- Test serum 25(OH)D if supplementing chronically
- Pair with K2 (MK-7, ~90 mcg) if also taking calcium

### Magnesium Glycinate
- 200–400mg at night
- Helps sleep, recovery, stress
- Glycinate or citrate forms (oxide is poorly absorbed)

### Omega-3s (Fish Oil)
- 1–2g combined EPA+DHA daily
- Supports general health, inflammation, skin
- Higher quality matters — cheap fish oil is rancid

## Pre-Workout Stack (DIY)

A good performance stack:
- Caffeine 200mg
- L-theanine 100–200mg (smooths the caffeine jitter)
- Citrulline malate 6–8g (pump and endurance)
- Alpha-GPC 300mg (focus — optional)
- Electrolytes
- 30–50g carbs

Cheaper and cleaner than most branded pre-workouts.

## Tier 3 — Optional, Real Tradeoffs

- Ashwagandha (cortisol/stress; mixed evidence)
- Collagen peptides (skin, joints; ~10g/day)
- L-glutamine (overrated for natty lifters)
- Multivitamin (insurance, not magic)

Use only if they solve a specific problem.

## Don't Bother

- "Natural test boosters" (ZMA, fenugreek, tribulus, etc.) — don't move test in healthy lifters
- Turkesterone — meta-analyses are unimpressed
- Random "anabolic" blends — proprietary blends with under-dosed ingredients
- Shilajit from sketchy sources — heavy metal contamination risk
- "BCAAs" if you're hitting protein from food — already in your protein

If a supplement promises steroid-like results without being a steroid, it's cope.

## Safety Notes

- Don't megadose anything
- Don't stack 10+ supplements — interactions multiply
- Talk to a doctor if you're under 18, pregnant, on medication, or have a health condition
- Avoid research chemicals (MK-677, LGD-4033, etc.) — real risks, not approved for human use
