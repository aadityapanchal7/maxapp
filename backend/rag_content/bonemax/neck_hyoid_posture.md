# Neck, Hyoid & Posture

A strong neck and clean posture do more for your facial frame than almost any single facial intervention. Forward-head posture hides the jawline, drops the tongue off the palate, and shortens the visual length of the chin.

## Why The Neck Matters Aesthetically

- Strong traps and a thick neck make the head look proportionate to the frame
- Posture sets the angle between your jaw and clavicle — that angle IS the jawline in photos
- Hyoid position (the bone above the larynx) affects how defined the under-chin area looks
- A weak neck on a muscular body looks "pinhead"; a strong neck on an average body looks athletic

## Posture — The Daily Drill

- **Chin tucks**: 3 sets of 10. Slide the chin straight back like a drawer, no tilting.
- **Wall posture hold**: heels, butt, shoulders, head touching the wall. Hold 30–60 seconds. 1–2× daily.
- **Shoulder retractions**: pull shoulder blades down and back, hold 5 seconds, release. 2 sets of 10.
- **Thoracic extension**: foam roller across the upper back, gentle extension, 1–2 minutes daily.
- **Deep nasal breathing**: 5 minutes a day reinforces tongue-on-palate posture.

## Neck Training Protocol

Light, slow, controlled. The neck has a lot of small joints and no margin for jerky reps.

- **Neck curls** (lying on back, lift head 1 inch off ground): 2×15
- **Neck extensions** (lying face-down on a bench, head off the edge): 2×15
- **Side isometrics**: hand on side of head, push gently, hold 10 seconds × 3 each side
- **Trap shrugs**: 3×12–15, controlled tempo

Frequency: 2–3×/week. More is not better. Stop immediately if dizzy, numb, or you get headaches.

## Under-Chin Definition

- Tongue-on-palate posture
- Chin tucks
- Neck training
- Lower body fat
- Debloat routine (especially before photos)
- Avoid scrolling for hours with the head dropped — it compresses the submental area daily

## Photo Posture

- Neck long, ears stacked over shoulders
- Chin slightly forward and slightly down
- Shoulders relaxed, slight scapular retraction
- Tongue on palate (you can feel the under-chin firm up)
- Slight squint to add tension to the eye area
