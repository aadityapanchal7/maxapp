# Sleep, IGF-1 & Recovery

The face you see in the mirror is mostly a sleep diagnostic. Bad sleep tanks IGF-1, raises cortisol, drives water retention, and depletes skin barrier function — all of which read on the face within 24 h.

## Why Sleep Drives Facial Aesthetics

- **Growth hormone pulses** during deep sleep support skin and bone repair
- **IGF-1** stays in a healthy range only with adequate sleep + protein
- **Cortisol** drops overnight; chronic short sleep keeps it elevated → facial puffiness, breakouts, fat redistribution
- **Lymphatic clearance** of the brain and face happens during deep sleep
- **Skin barrier repair** peaks in the second half of the night

## Sleepmaxxing For The Face

- 7–9 hours, the same window every night (±30 min)
- Dark room, blackout if needed; even small light exposure suppresses melatonin
- Cool room (65–68°F / 18–20°C) — face fluid drains better when you're not overheating
- Nasal breathing all night (treat congestion or sleep apnea if mouth-breathing chronically)
- Manage allergies — nighttime histamine release puffs the under-eye
- Last meal 2–3 h before bed; no high-salt late dinners
- No alcohol within 4 h of bed — destroys deep sleep even if you "sleep through it"

## Training For Facial Structure

Resistance training raises IGF-1 acutely and supports bone density long-term.

- 3–4 strength sessions per week
- Compound lifts: squat, deadlift, bench, row, overhead press
- Add neck and trap work 2×/week (separate or as a finisher)
- Sprint or conditioning work 1–2×/week (helps lymph and body comp)
- Avoid chronic overtraining — it crashes recovery and shows on the face

## Recovery Math

- Calories at maintenance or slight surplus if growing muscle
- Protein at 0.8–1 g/lb daily
- Hydration: 2–3 L water; more if training hard or in heat
- Electrolytes if you sweat heavily — sodium + potassium balance matters
- 1–2 full rest days per week, no exceptions

## When Sleep Won't Cooperate

- Audit caffeine — last cup before noon
- Audit screens — dim and de-blue 90 minutes before bed
- Audit room — cool, dark, quiet, phone outside the room
- If you've nailed all that and still can't sleep, talk to a doctor about sleep apnea (especially if you snore or wake up gasping). Untreated apnea ages the face fast.
