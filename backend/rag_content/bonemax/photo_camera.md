# Photo & Camera Maxxing

Most "before/after" transformations on social media are 80% better photography. Lighting, angle, lens, and timing change a face dramatically. Learn the controllable variables.

## Lighting

- **Natural diffuse light** (overcast day, near a window): the most flattering for skin and features
- **Side lighting** at 45°: sculpts cheekbones and the jaw shadow
- **Golden hour** (last hour before sunset / first hour after sunrise): warm, low-angle, forgiving on texture and color
- **Avoid overhead lighting**: drops shadows under the eyes and into the smile lines — adds 10 visual years
- **Avoid direct flash from the front**: flattens everything

## Angles

- **Slightly above eye level**: softens the face, larger eyes, smaller jaw — good for "softer" looks
- **Slightly below eye level**: stronger jaw, more dominance, projected chin
- **Direct eye level**: most natural; default for honest photos
- **3/4 angle** (face turned ~30° from camera): activates the cheekbone shadow on the visible side
- **Side profile**: shows ogee curve, jaw projection, neck angle — most diagnostic angle for structure

## Lens & Distance

- Phone front cameras are wide-angle: distort features when held close — back up an arm's length minimum
- 50mm equivalent or longer = most natural facial proportions
- Don't take serious selfies on the front camera if you can avoid it; ask someone to use the back camera instead

## Pre-Photo Routine

- 8+ hours sleep night before
- Low sodium for 24 h
- 2–3 L water the day before
- Cold splash + light gua sha morning of
- Caffeine eye serum 30 min before
- Light meal 90 min before — full enough to look healthy, not bloated
- Walk for 10 min before to flush lymph

## Posture For The Shot

- Neck long, ears stacked over shoulders
- Chin slightly forward and down (the "owl" head tilt to find the right angle)
- Slight squint to add tension to the eye area ("sprezzatura")
- Tongue on the palate firms the under-chin
- Relaxed shoulders, slight scapular retraction

## What Wrecks Photos

- Phone held below the chin (under-chin shadow, double chin even when there isn't one)
- Phone held above the head (childlike, loses jawline)
- Smiling fake-wide (cheek puffiness obscures cheekbones)
- Hair plastered to the forehead (no breathing room around the face)
- Plain wall directly behind (no depth — face looks flat)
