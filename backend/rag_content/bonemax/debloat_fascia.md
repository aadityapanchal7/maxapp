# Debloat & Fascia

Most "I look bad today" days are not bone or skin — they're fluid. The face holds water like a sponge. Reduce that, and a sharper face appears overnight.

## What Causes Facial Puffiness

- Sodium spikes (fast food, chips, processed dinners)
- Alcohol — both the alcohol and the disrupted sleep
- Poor sleep (or sleeping flat without elevation)
- Allergies and sinus inflammation
- Crying, stress, cortisol spikes
- Lymphatic stagnation from a sedentary day
- High-carb late-night meals (less than salt, but real)
- Intense workouts that spike cortisol short-term

## Daily Debloat Protocol

- **Cold splash or cold roller**: 30–60 seconds on the face, focusing on cheeks and under-eyes. Constricts vessels, drops puffiness fast.
- **Hydration**: counter-intuitive, but dehydration triggers fluid retention. 2–3 L water daily.
- **Morning walk**: 10–15 minutes activates lymphatic flow.
- **Gentle lymph drainage**: light strokes from cheek → ear → neck → collarbone. 1–2 minutes.
- **Sleep elevated**: extra pillow keeps fluid from pooling in the face overnight.

## Lymphatic Drainage Basics

- Drain DOWNWARD only — toward the collarbone
- Light pressure — you're moving lymph just under the skin, not deep tissue
- Behind the ears, down the sides of the neck, finishing at the collarbone
- Skip if you have skin irritation, active acne flare, or recent dental work

## Gua Sha & Roller Safety

- Light pressure only — you should never see redness that lasts more than 20 minutes
- 2–3 minutes per session, 3–5×/week
- Use a slip product (oil or moisturizer) — never drag dry
- Move outward and downward, never against the muscle direction
- Stop if it stings, bruises, or breaks out new acne

## Pre-Photo Debloat (24 h prep)

- Cap sodium at ~2g for the day
- No alcohol
- 8+ hours sleep, head elevated
- Morning: cold splash → light gua sha → caffeine eye serum → walk for 15 min
- Photo time: 3–4 hours after waking, after light eating, before any salt
