# Chin & Lower Third

The chin sets the lower-face balance. A weak or recessed chin makes the rest of the face look softer than it actually is. A pronounced chin pulls the whole side profile forward.

## What Drives Chin Appearance

- Bone projection (genetic; surgical or filler change only in adults)
- Dental bite — overbite or recessed mandible affects chin read
- Submental fat ("double chin") — biggest day-to-day variable
- Tongue and neck posture — forward-head posture buries the chin
- Skin tightness under the jaw

## Safe Chin Improvement

- **Posture**: chin slightly tucked, neck long. The same chin reads stronger from a tall posture.
- **Tongue posture**: pressed to the palate keeps the submental area toned over years.
- **Neck training**: slow chin-tucks, neck curls, side isometrics. Reduces the "soft under-chin" look.
- **Body composition**: most "weak chins" are actually 17–20% body fat hiding a normal mandible.
- **Orthodontic evaluation** if your bite genuinely sits behind your upper teeth — a clinician can stage corrective treatment.

## Chin Fraudmaxxes

- Light contour just under the lower lip suggests projection
- Highlight on the center of the chin pulls it forward in light
- Beard or stubble shaping along the chin point
- Camera slightly below eye level adds projection
- Avoid heavy-lower-face puffiness (sodium, late alcohol) before photos

## What NOT To Do

- No "chin smashing"
- No DIY pressure devices
- No clenching routines aimed at "pulling the chin forward"
- No mouth-tape regimens marketed as chin-projection fixes

The chin is structurally fixed in adults. You're improving how it reads, not how big it is.
