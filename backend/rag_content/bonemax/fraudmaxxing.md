# Fraudmaxxing

Fraudmaxxing is visual enhancement without structural change. Makeup, grooming, lighting, posing, styling. It's safe, reversible, and takes the same face from a 6 to an 8 in photos. Treat it as a serious skill, not a cope.

## What Counts

- Subtle contouring (cream or matte powder, light hand)
- Strategic highlighter placement
- Brow shaping and tinting
- Beard / stubble lineup
- Hairstyle volume and shape balancing
- Camera angle and lighting
- Posture and facial expression in photos
- Color theory in clothing

## Cheekbone Fraudmaxxes

- Matte bronzer right under the cheek hollow, blended toward the corner of the mouth
- Highlighter on the top of the cheekbone (subtle satin, not glitter)
- Blush above the cheekbone for a flushed read
- Red tinted balm placed high on the cheekbone for a "natural blush"
- Matte finish on the lower cheek so it doesn't compete with the highlight

## Eye Area Fraudmaxxes

- Eyeliner along the upper lash line deepens orbital contrast
- Brow gel + tint = framed, intentional brows
- Color corrector + concealer for under-eyes
- Caffeine serum 30 min before photos
- Slight outer-corner shadow extends the eye visually

## Nose Fraudmaxxes

- Subtle side contour to suggest a straighter bridge
- Bridge highlight to elongate visually
- Oil control on the nose tip and bridge (matte = less attention)
- Tinted sunscreen reduces redness on/around the nose
- Address blackheads gently — visible texture pulls focus to the nose

## Jaw Fraudmaxxes

- Beard or stubble shaping along the mandible angle
- Neckline cleanup (sharp boundary between hair and skin)
- Light contour right under the jaw shadow
- Lower-face debloat 24 h before photos
- Camera slightly below eye level

## Hair Fraudmaxxes

- Volume on top to balance a wide face; flatter sides to slim a round face
- Hairline styling (avoid styles that expose temple thinning if applicable)
- Side profile silhouette matters more than people think — check it in a 3-mirror setup
- Don't chase trends that fight your face shape

## What Fraudmaxxing Is NOT

- Photoshop or AI editing your real photos for dating apps (will backfire on first meet)
- Filters that change underlying proportions
- Anything you can't replicate live in the situation that matters
