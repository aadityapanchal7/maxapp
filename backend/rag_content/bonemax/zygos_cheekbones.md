# Zygos & Cheekbones

The zygos are the cheekbones. They control the ogee curve, midface width, and how dramatically light and shadow play across the upper face. Prominent zygos are a top-three driver of perceived facial harmony.

## What Actually Makes Cheekbones Look Sharp

- Underlying bone width and projection (mostly genetic)
- Low puffiness — water retention washes them out fast
- Skin tightness over the zygomatic arch
- Body fat low enough to expose the bone shadow
- Lighting and angle

You cannot grow zygomatic bone in adults. You can dramatically change how visible existing bone is.

## Safe Cheekbone Enhancement

- **Debloat**: cut sodium spikes, reduce alcohol, sleep elevated, stay hydrated. Morning puffiness is the biggest day-to-day saboteur.
- **Body composition**: 12–15% body fat exposes structure without looking depleted.
- **Posture**: chin slightly tucked elongates the neck and frames the cheekbone shadow.
- **Skin quality**: smooth, tight skin reads sharper. Sunscreen and a basic retinoid over years matter more than any massage.
- **Gentle facial massage / gua sha**: light pressure for 2–3 minutes, 3–5×/week. Drains lymph, does not move bone.

Avoid: heavy gua sha, "bone smashing," hammering devices, hard pressure rolling. These bruise tissue and inflame the area, making it look puffier, not sharper.

## Hollow-Cheek Illusion (fraudmaxx)

- Matte bronzer right under the cheek hollow — start at the ear and feather toward the corner of the mouth, stop above the smile line
- Highlight on the top of the cheekbone catches light
- Avoid shiny products in the lower cheek — they fill the shadow you want to keep
- Slight beard shadow or stubble on the lower face deepens the contrast

## Lighting & Angles For Zygos

- Side-lit photos sculpt the cheekbone shadow naturally
- 3/4 angle with the chin slightly down emphasizes ogee curve
- Avoid flash directly in front — it flattens everything
- Golden-hour light is the cheapest cheekbone upgrade you'll ever get
