# Safety Rules & Red Flags

Bonemaxxing is mostly safe when it stays in the lane of posture, sleep, nutrition, debloat, controlled chewing, and grooming. The risky failures all share a pattern: someone tries to force structural change with mechanical pressure, off-label drugs, or extreme protocols.

## Stop Immediately If

- Jaw clicking gets worse, or you start hearing it for the first time
- TMJ pain, especially after starting chewing or jaw exercises
- Headaches triggered by chewing
- Neck dizziness or numbness during/after neck training
- Skin irritation or burning from a "fraudmaxx" product
- Numbness, tingling, or persistent swelling anywhere on the face
- Bruising that doesn't fade in 3–4 days

## See A Professional For

- **Dentist or orthodontist**: bite that doesn't close evenly, recessed jaw, persistent jaw pain, suspected bruxism
- **ENT**: chronic mouth breathing, deviated septum signs, snoring with daytime fatigue (possible apnea)
- **Sleep specialist**: snoring + waking gasping + daytime sleepiness → suspect obstructive sleep apnea, which prematurely ages the face
- **Dermatologist**: persistent redness, acne that doesn't respond to OTC routines, pigmentation concerns
- **Endocrinologist**: persistent fatigue, hormonal symptoms, considering supplement protocols

## Never Do

- Hit, smash, or use percussion devices on facial bones
- DIY chin or jaw "expansion" devices
- Topical steroids on the face for non-medical reasons
- Research chemicals (MK-677, LGD, etc.) for "facial growth"
- Stack 6+ supplements without bloodwork
- Mouth-tape if you have any sleep apnea suspicion
- Aggressive gua sha on inflamed or broken skin
- Anything sold with the promise of "growing bone in adults"

## Age & Development Notes

- Under 18: most structural changes from posture, breathing, and chewing happen here. Avoid supplements beyond a basic multivitamin/D unless prescribed.
- Pregnant or breastfeeding: skip new supplement protocols and clinical-strength actives entirely.
- On medication: every supplement interacts with something. Run new protocols past your prescriber.
- Existing health conditions: talk to your clinician before changing diet, training, or supplement routines.

## The One-Sentence Rule

If a method causes pain, swelling, numbness, or visible damage, it's an injury — not a transformation.
