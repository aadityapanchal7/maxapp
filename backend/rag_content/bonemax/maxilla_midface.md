# Maxilla & Midface

The maxilla is the upper jaw. It carries the nose, supports the cheeks and under-eye area, and sets the dental arch shape. A more forward-positioned maxilla makes the whole face look more sculpted and balanced.

## What Influences Midface Development

- Resting tongue posture (tongue on palate, lips sealed, teeth lightly together)
- Nasal breathing day and night
- Head and neck posture
- Dental alignment and bite
- Chewing load through normal foods

Most of the real change happens before facial sutures close in the late teens. Adults still benefit from posture and tone improvements but should not expect bone movement without orthognathic surgery.

## Mewing — Resting Tongue Posture

- Tip of tongue behind the upper front teeth, not touching them
- Whole tongue suctioned flat to the palate, not just the tip
- Teeth lightly together, lips sealed, breathe through your nose
- Swallow with the tongue pressing the palate, not pushing off the teeth
- Consistency over intensity — gentle pressure all day, never force

Avoid: clenching the jaw, pushing teeth forward with the tongue, or "hard mewing" with deliberate force. Those cause pain and dental issues, not maxilla growth.

## Nasal Breathing

Mouth breathing pulls the head forward, drops the tongue off the palate, and undoes posture work overnight.

- Confirm you can breathe through both nostrils — see an ENT for chronic congestion, deviated septum, or sleep apnea symptoms.
- Treat allergies and sinus issues before relying on tape or strips.
- For nighttime mouth breathers: address the cause first; mouth tape only if cleared by a clinician.

## Posture's Role In Midface

Forward-head posture rotates the cervical spine and tips the chin down. The midface looks recessed even when the bone is normal.

- Chin tucks 2–3 sets of 10 daily
- Wall posture drill: stand against a wall with heels, butt, shoulders, head touching, hold 30–60 seconds
- Screen at eye level. Lower screens are the main driver of forward-head posture.

## Visual Boost For Midface (fraudmaxxing)

- Light contour under the cheek hollow to suggest forward-grown zygos
- Highlight on the upper cheek plane catches light and pulls the midface forward
- Subtle blush above the cheekbone for a flushed, healthy read
- Hairstyle volume balanced to avoid making the midface look narrow
