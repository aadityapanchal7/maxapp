# Brow Ridge & Eye Area

Brow ridge shape is mostly genetic. The eye area, by contrast, changes daily based on sleep, fluid retention, allergies, and skin condition. That's where the leverage is.

## What Makes Eyes Read Strong

- Tight, even under-eye tone (no purple/grey hollow)
- Defined orbital shadow (not puffy, not sunken)
- Clean brow shape framing the eye
- Lash density and brow density
- "Hunter eyes" — the result of a brow ridge sitting close to the lash line, plus a slight squint

## Safe Eye Area Enhancement

- **Sleep**: 7–9 hours, consistent time. Single biggest variable for under-eye color.
- **Allergy control**: chronic allergies darken under-eyes via constant rubbing and venous pooling. Treat the cause.
- **Sunscreen daily**: UV is the main driver of eye-area aging.
- **Caffeine eye serum**: vasoconstricts the under-eye in 10–20 minutes; great pre-photo trick.
- **Cold compress** in the morning: 30–60 seconds reduces puffiness fast.
- **Gentle retinoid** (start 2×/week, ramp slowly) to thicken the skin under the eye over months.

## What NOT To Do

- Hit, press, or "smash" the brow ridge — risk of fracture and nerve damage
- Apply hydrocortisone to the eye area off-label (skin thinning, glaucoma risk)
- Aggressive tugging while applying or removing eye products
- Daily harsh exfoliation right under the eye

## Brow Fraudmaxxes

- Brow tinting evens the brow color and adds visual density
- Brow gel sets the hairs in a slight upward sweep — instantly more defined
- Slight darkening at the outer brow tail extends the eye visually
- Matte shadow in the orbital crease deepens the eye socket

## Under-Eye Tactics

- Color corrector (peach for blue/purple under-eyes) before concealer
- Concealer one shade lighter than skin, blended out, no powder if you have texture
- Avoid heavy concealer if dehydration lines are present — it cakes
- Cold spoon or roller in the morning for puff reduction
