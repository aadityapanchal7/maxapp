# Jawline Engineering

A defined jawline is mostly the combination of mandible shape, masseter size, low neck/face fat, and skin tightness. Most "jawline transformations" you see online are body-fat changes plus better lighting, not bone growth.

## What Defines A Sharp Jawline

- Mandible angle and length (genetic)
- Masseter muscle size (trainable, with care)
- Neck and submental fat (diet + body comp)
- Skin tightness along the inferior border
- Posture — forward-head posture buries the lower face

## Safe Chewing & Masseter Training

The masseter responds to load like any muscle. The protocol is conservative because TMJ injuries are slow to heal.

**Starter protocol**:
- 5–10 minutes of mastic gum (or another firm sugar-free gum), 2–3 times per week
- Chew evenly on both sides — alternate sides every minute
- Stop immediately if jaw clicks worsen, you get headaches, or the joint locks
- Increase only if the previous week was completely pain-free

**Progression**:
- Week 1–2: 5 min × 2/week
- Week 3–4: 10 min × 3/week
- Beyond: cap at 15 min × 4/week. More is not better — overtraining the masseter creates a square jaw that may or may not be the look you want.

**TMJ safety rules**:
- No daily overtraining
- No jaw clenching exercises
- No "jaw exerciser" devices if you have any history of TMJ pain
- See a dentist or orthodontist if your bite feels off or pain persists

## What NOT To Do

- No chin smashing
- No DIY pressure devices
- No mouthguards used for "facial remodeling" without a clinician
- No hard clenching routines
- No promises that jaw exercise will move the mandible

## Jawline Fraudmaxxes

- Beard or stubble lineup along the mandible amplifies the existing angle
- Neckline grooming (clean shave below the jaw, no hair on the upper neck) creates the "edge"
- Light contour right under the jawline shadow
- Camera slightly below eye level strengthens the chin and jaw read
- Drop sodium 24 h before photos to deflate the lower face

## Underlying Drivers Beat Tricks

If you're 18% body fat with forward-head posture, no amount of contour or chewing will give you a sharp jawline. Cut to 12–15%, fix posture, then layer in masseter work and grooming.
