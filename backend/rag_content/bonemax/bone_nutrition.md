# Bone Nutrition & Micronutrients

Bone is metabolically active tissue. Even in adults — past the peak growth window — nutrition affects density, mineralization, and the rate of facial bone resorption with age. Get the basics right; skip the megadose stacks.

## Core Nutrients For Bone Health

- **Protein**: ~0.8–1 g per lb of bodyweight daily. Bone is one-third protein matrix.
- **Vitamin D**: 1000–2000 IU/day if sun exposure is limited; test serum 25(OH)D if you supplement chronically.
- **Calcium**: 1000–1200 mg/day, ideally from food. Excess supplemental calcium has cardiovascular tradeoffs.
- **Magnesium**: 300–400 mg/day. Most diets are short. Glycinate or citrate forms are best tolerated.
- **Vitamin K2 (MK-7)**: 90–180 mcg/day. Helps direct calcium into bone instead of soft tissue.
- **Zinc**: 8–11 mg/day from food; supplemental zinc above 25 mg/day for prolonged periods can crash copper.
- **Boron**: educational — 3 mg/day shows modest bone-density benefit in studies. Not a transformation.

## Food-First Sources

- Dairy (or fortified alternatives) — calcium, protein, often vitamin D
- Eggs — vitamin D, choline, complete protein
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines) — vitamin D, K2, omega-3
- Meat or legumes — protein, zinc, iron
- Leafy greens (kale, collards) — vitamin K, magnesium, calcium
- Nuts and seeds — magnesium, healthy fats
- Fruit (especially citrus, berries) — vitamin C for collagen synthesis

## Supplement Hierarchy (if you're going to use any)

1. Vitamin D if your blood level is below ~30 ng/mL or sun exposure is minimal
2. Magnesium glycinate at night if you don't eat leafy greens daily
3. K2 (MK-7) only if you're taking calcium or D supplements
4. Whey or plant protein if you can't hit the protein target from food

## What To Avoid

- **MK-677**: research chemical, not approved for human use, real cardiovascular and metabolic risks
- **Megadosing single nutrients** (e.g. 50,000 IU vitamin D weekly without bloodwork)
- **Zinc above 40 mg/day** without copper, for extended periods
- **Stacking 10+ supplements** — the marginal benefit diminishes fast and interactions multiply
- **Hormone manipulation supplements** marketed for "facial growth" in adults

## When To Talk To A Doctor

- Under 18, pregnant, breastfeeding, or on chronic medication
- Existing bone-density issues, fracture history, or family osteoporosis
- Considering anything stronger than a basic multivitamin
- Unusual fatigue, bone pain, or mood changes after starting a new supplement
